Federal Oligarchs' Council Proposes to Eliminate Ministry of Education

Washington, United American Federation. After a lengthy meeting, the Federal Oligarchs' Council has decided to recommend that Congress immediately proceed with the elimination of the Ministry of Education in order to significantly reduce federal budget expenditures. This step is driven by the serious situation surrounding the country's financial system, which is striving to overcome growing deficits.

According to official sources, this radical move will free up substantial funds needed for maintaining key state projects such as road construction, infrastructure development, and military support. Experts emphasize the need for tough measures since the country is experiencing deep economic challenges caused by prolonged financial crises.

"We believe it is necessary to take urgent steps to optimize the use of budgetary resources," said Howard Hughes, chairman of the council. "Education remains an important element of national development, but now it must give priority to other areas."

Opponents warn that eliminating the ministry would have catastrophic consequences for the UAF's educational system, especially for its most vulnerable segments. However, supporters argue that private education can provide sufficient quality services and accessibility even for the poorest citizens through tax breaks and subsidies.

The public is currently in shock and confusion over impending changes. Calls are already being made for mass protest demonstrations against government actions, although officials assure that these changes are inevitable and essential for preserving the economy amid global financial turmoil.

The next congressional session where the proposal will be considered is scheduled for next week. Public opinion is likely to become a decisive factor in making the final decision.
